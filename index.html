THE PROBLEM

Siloed Monitoring, Cascading Disasters

Seismologists track earthquakes. Volcanologists watch eruptions. Meteorologists forecast floods. But catastrophic events don't respect these boundaries ‚Äî an earthquake triggers tsunamis and volcanic unrest, wildfire seasons strain emergency response during pandemics, and a geomagnetic storm can disable the very communications infrastructure needed to coordinate it all.

The data already exists across USGS, NASA FIRMS, NOAA, WHO, and dozens of other public APIs. But no single platform aggregates these signals, identifies cross-domain connections, or predicts how disasters compound in real time. That gap costs lives.

OUR APPROACH

One Platform. Every Threat. Real Time.

!(Not)Doomsday pulls live data from 8+ authoritative APIs, runs each through domain-specific risk analysis pipelines, then feeds all active threats simultaneously into a Groq-powered LLM (Llama 3.3 70B) backed by 176,000 historical disaster records in ChromaDB. The result: composite risk scores, cascade predictions, and coordinated response recommendations ‚Äî all visualized on an interactive 3D globe.

THE AI BRAIN

Cascade Prediction Engine

No human expert can monitor six threat domains simultaneously, identify cross-domain connections, and generate coordinated response plans in real time. Our cascade engine takes all active threats, retrieves the most relevant historical precedents from a 176,000-document ChromaDB, and uses Groq's Llama 3.3 70B to predict how disasters cascade ‚Äî with timelines, probabilities, and specific recommended actions.
